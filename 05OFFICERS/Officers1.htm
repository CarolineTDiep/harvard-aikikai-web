The Co-Presidents

 
 

Maciej Godlewski (gokyu), '07, started aikido in 2003 during his first year at Harvard. Sice joining the club, he has also served as club Secretary, attended seminars at Cornell and MIT, and successfully tested for the rank of gokyu early in 2005. He also participated in last year's Spring Arts First demonstration, performing techniques from his then-recent gokyu test.

While not in class, Maciej busies himself with his History and Science concentration classes, and by climbing up to his lonely 4th floor room in Eliot house N Entryway.

Michael Swita (gokyu), '07, also started aikido in 2003 during his first year at Harvard. He also lives with Maciej. Convenient for club business, huh? During the winter of 2005, Michael successfully tested for the rank of gokyu, awarded to him by Dick Stroud-sensei of the MIT Aikido Club, and Sioux Hall-sensei of Harvard Aikikai. He previously served as club Treasurer.

Apart from Aikido, Mike is also involved with the Harvard Hapkido Club, Magic Society, and Harvard Polish Society on top of dealing with the rigors of his Biochemical Sciences concentration. He does not really have blond hair.

The Veep

Corey Masao Johnson (shodan), '07, is currently building this webpage. Right now. Corey started Aikido in 1996 under the direction of Barbara and Robert Klein-sensei of Aikido of Hilo, Hawaii. He entered Harvard in 2003 as an ikkyu, and successfully tested for the rank of shodan under Klein-sensei in 2004. He practices regularly with the MIT Aikido Club in addition to Harvard Aikikai, and organized Harvard's Spring Seminar last year. He is in his second term as club VP.

Outside of Aikido classes, he is active in the Harvard Hawaii Club and the Harvard Advocate. He is currently spending a semester abroad in Kyoto, training with the Kyoto University Aikido Club and the Kyoto Budo Center Aikido Dojo.

The President Emeritus

Zhe Lu (sankyu), '05, started aikido in 2001 as a first-year at Harvard, and later served two years as club President. During his presidency, the club successfully received a $5000 grant for new tatami, and began its search for a new permanent practice space. He was also a driving force behind the club's alignment under the Harvard University Reischauer Institute for Japanese Studies, and was essential to the success of Harvard Aikikai's first spring seminar. He tested for the rank of sankyu in winter 2005.

In June of 2005 he graduated from Harvard with an A.B. and M.A. in Chemistry, and is currently in his first year as a PhD student at MIT, where he practices under the direction of Dick Stroud-sensei.
