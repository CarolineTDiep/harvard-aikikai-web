About Aikido:
 
 
   

Aikido is a Japanese martial art that is defined as the way to harmonize with the universal force of nature. Aikido was developed by Morihei Ueshiba, a Japanese warrior and master of the arts of Jujutsu, Kendo or sword arts, and Sojutsu or spear arts. Morihei Ueshiba, or O Sensei (Great Teacher), survived great hardships and tragedies as a youth. O Sensei's determination not to be defeated or weakened in life or battle led him to blend his martial training with his burgeoning spirituality. O Sensei's developing spirituality resulted in him questioning traditional Budo. Though undefeated in his battles with masters of other martial arts, O Sensei questioned the wisdom of meeting force with force. Surely, he believed, someone bigger and stronger would ultimately defeat the greatest master of any martial art thus rendering the life work of such masters meaningless. O Sensei understood that life's battles were many, both mundane and serious. Victory over such battles required that the individual be developed, not just the individual's martial skills. It is only through developing the self beyond selfish, petty ambition that the individual is released to realize his or her full potential. O Sensei believed martial arts training could be a means to fully developing one's potential. Sincere and rigorous training in the tactics and techniques of Aikido then serves two purposes: self-defense and individual growth.

The dynamic practice of Aikido and Aikido technique requires the practitioner to move in a circular manner with a strong center. One way to picture this is to consider the hurricane. The eye of the storm is calm, but circling around the center of the storm are powerful winds. The essence of Aikido is for the practitioner to remain calm and to blend with an attack while adding one's inner power or ki to the spherical motion. Blending with an attack and the spherical motion of Aikido creates a powerful dynamic that can decisively end a conflict. In addition to these larger movements, there are also smaller movements that serve to deflect attacks and immobilize attackers. Both the larger and smaller movements of Aikido utilize natural forces or the laws of physics to end physical conflicts. The circular movements create centrifugal and centripetal forces. Other movements combine inertia, gravity and leverage to achieve the desired result. The Aikido practitioner combines all of these forces, movements and techniques in harmony with the natural world, and in doing so successfully, ends the conflict and achieves harmony. These same concepts can also be used in non-physical conflicts to achieve desired results.
